Role Management

  • Open the Settings tab, then the Role Settings tab. This displays the table of roles, and the permissions that this role has on the various resources, such as "create", "read", etc.
    You can add a new role by selecting the "+Add Role" button on the top lefthand side of the page.
    When you click the "+Add Role" button, there are a few fields that you must fill out. The first is the name of the role, such as "guest" or "user1", and the second is an optional description of the role. The third field is a "Resource" dropdown that allows you to select the resource type, and the permissions for that resource.
    Once you have selected a resource, click the "+Add Resource" button to select the permissions that this role will have for the resource. For example, a role may have "read" access to the "cases" resource. You may select multiple permissions for a resource, but some resources will only have certain permissions available for it. For example, a role cannot have "execute" permissions on the "app_apis" resource, because there is no "execute" permission for that resource.
    After you have selected all of the permissions for a resource, you can select another resource from the same dropdown and click the "+Add Resource" button again to setup permissions for another resource.
    When you are done editing the new role, click the "Add Role" button at the bottom of the window to create the role.
    After a role is created, you will see information about it on the "Role Settings" page, such as the role name, and its permissions. You also have the ability to edit the role by clicking the pencil icon on the right side of the page, as well as the ability to delete a role by clicking the red "x" on the right side of the page.
